### Executive Summamry: 
The problem I was trying to solve was, “How can I make advanced math and computer science concepts (like probabilistic algorithms) more accessible and easier for high school students to understand?”

I solved this problem by creating a paper that took an advanced CS and math topic (in my case, Monte Carlo Localization) and explained the topic in extreme depth by lowering the level without losing precision. I also paired these explanations with C++ code.

### Update:
Since my last portfolio update, I have finished my Monte Carlo Localization (MCL) paper, particularly expanding from the foundational chapters into the later sections.

The most significant development is that I have moved beyond just the initial setup sections (introduction, purpose, guide to reading, and uniform particle distribution) and have now finished the sections on state sampling, updating weights, raycasting, estimating position, and resampling.

In addition, I have added code supplements to each section so no math is unaccompanied, along with having a code appendix at the end. 

Lastly, I have gone back and added more in-depth explanation to the earlier sections
